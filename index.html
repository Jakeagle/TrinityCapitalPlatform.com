Build Real-World Financial Skills

Trinity Capital combines realistic banking simulation with interactive lessons to give students hands-on experience managing money, understanding transactions, and making smart financial decisions. Teachers get powerful tools to track progress, assign lessons, and monitor class and individual student financial health.

  • Two Complete Platforms for Full Learning

    The Student App provides a realistic banking interface with simulated accounts, transactions, bills management, and interactive lessons. The Teacher Dashboard lets educators create lessons, assign units, monitor student health metrics, track progress, and communicate with parentsâ€”all in one place.

  • Comprehensive Financial Education

    Beyond basic banking simulation, Trinity Capital includes income and spending analysis, detailed transaction categorization, bills and paychecks management, money transfers between accounts, peer-to-peer payments, and lesson-based learning conditions that require students to use the app to progress through units.

Trinity Capital is the comprehensive solution schools need to prepare students for financial success. With realistic banking simulation, detailed financial health dashboards, and powerful teacher management tools, Trinity Capital transforms financial literacy from abstract concepts into actionable skills students actually use.

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

Do bills and payments appear at realistic times?

Yes they do! Trinity Capital is built to provide the most realistic experience as possible. That means when you submit a bill or a paycheck in the simulation, it hits your account when youd expect it to.

Will there be lessons included in the app?

Yes there will be! We are working hard to transform Trinity Capital into a full fledged financial literacy tool. We are working on lessons that will teach users about the basics of personal finance, and how to apply them in the real world.

What the heck is the demo? And why does it look so different from the paid app?

The demo was initially built to show public schools what apps like Trinity Capital can do. But now, I am releasing it to the public so they can try out Trinity Capital for free. As for the look? We had to simplify the demo to make it usable in a classroom setting. We'll get around to updating it here soon.
