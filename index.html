F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

Do bills and payments appear at realistic times?

Yes they do! Trinity Capital is built to provide the most realistic experience as possible. That means when you submit a bill or a paycheck in the simulation, it hits your account when youd expect it to.

Why dont you teach a lot on credit?

The Trinity Capital team acknowledges that credit is a part of the financial world and if handled correctly, will not cause harm to people in the real world. With that said, we do not believe that credit should be used in any context, and any situation where credit can be used, there is always a more viable cash reserve option that doesnt put people at risk of going into debt. We teach credit in a matter of fact way, but do not support its use.

What is your lesson content based off of?

The framework for our lessons is from the Texas Education Agency's personal financial literacy course TEKS. TEKS are bassically everything youre supposed to learn in a class. We teach these TEKS, with some changes for things such as credit, more effective budgeting, ext.

What the heck is the demo? And why does it look so different from the paid app?

The demo was initially built to show public schools what apps like Trinity Capital can do. But now, I am releasing it to the public so they can try out Trinity Capital for free. As for the look? We had to simplify the demo to make it usable in a classroom setting. We'll get around to updating it here soon.
